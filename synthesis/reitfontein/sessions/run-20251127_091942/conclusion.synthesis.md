### SLOT 8: Mining-Induced Seismicity Summary

Mining-induced seismicity is unlikely to trigger rupture of the entire 64 km Reitfontein Fault given the large 43 km distance from the South Deep Mine site and the depth difference between mining operations ($\leq$ 4 km) and typical natural seismogenic depths (5-16 km). The largest mining-related seismic event recorded in South Africa registered magnitude 5.3 in 2005. Mining-induced seismicity operates through two main mechanisms: collapse of highly stressed rock masses and shear along geological planes of weakness, driven by stress redistribution from excavation. The PSHA study excludes suspected mining-related events below M4.0 to minimize induced seismicity influence.
