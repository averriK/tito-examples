## SLOT 3: Reitfontein Fault Characterization Issues

The Reitfontein Fault exhibits multiple deficiencies in epistemic characterization that directly impact probabilistic seismic hazard assessment for return periods less than 10,000 years [KB:reitfontein.md]. The fault lacks documented geological or geomorphological evidence of neotectonic activity, with no observable surface ruptures, displaced young sediments, or geomorphic signs of recent faulting consistent with the standard definition of an active fault having produced movement within the last 35,000 years [KB:reitfontein.md][WEB:https://www.mdpi.com/2076-3263/14/3/68]. The absence of paleoseismic investigations represents a critical data gap, and Singh et al. (2009) specifically identified the Reitfontein Fault as requiring reassessment to determine current activity status, noting only minor present-day seismic activity along the structure [KB:reitfontein.md]. The assigned maximum magnitude of $M_{\text{max}} = 7.2$ derived from Wells and Coppersmith (1994) length-magnitude scaling relationships for a 64 km fault represents an unusually high value for the South African stable continental region, exceeding the largest recorded earthquake in South Africa (the 1969 Ceres event at $M_w$ 6.2) by 1.0 magnitude unit, equivalent to 32 times the seismic moment release [KB:reitfontein.md]. In stable continental regions, faults may behave episodically with ground-rupturing events recurring at intervals of thousands to hundreds of thousands of years, and paleoseismic studies emphasize the risks of relying solely on instrumental records in settings with such long recurrence intervals [KB:reitfontein.md][ARXIV:https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2018EGUGA..20.8378C]. However, the classification of the Reitfontein Fault as an active neotectonic structure with a slip rate of 1 m/Myr represents a conservative engineering assumption rather than demonstrated activity supported by empirical evidence, creating a fundamental mismatch between the fault's apparent paleoseismic character and its treatment in the hazard model as a source capable of influencing hazard at relatively short return periods [KB:reitfontein.md]. The 43 km distance from South Deep Mine and the depth separation between mining operations (≤4 km) and seismogenic depths (5-16 km for natural events) indicate that mining-induced triggering of full-fault rupture is highly unlikely, though stress perturbations from mining may contribute to reactivation of fault segments [KB:reitfontein.md].

## SLOT 4: Corrective Measures and Recommendations

The deficiencies in Reitfontein Fault characterization necessitate logic tree modifications to better represent epistemic uncertainty and reduce the systematic bias toward elevated hazard estimates [KB:reitfontein.md][KB:benchmark.md]. The most direct corrective measure involves eliminating the $M_{\text{max}} = 7.2$ branch entirely or assigning it a substantially reduced weight (≤0.10) within the logic tree framework to reflect the absence of empirical evidence supporting neotectonic activity within the last 35,000 years [KB:reitfontein.md][WEB:https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s00024-022-03143-4]. Alternative maximum magnitude branches should be introduced based on regional seismicity constraints, specifically incorporating values consistent with the largest recorded South African earthquake ($M_w$ 6.2) plus an appropriate uncertainty increment, yielding a more defensible range of $M_{\text{max}}$ values between 6.5 and 7.0 distributed across multiple branches with higher weights assigned to lower magnitudes [KB:benchmark.md][KB:reitfontein.md]. The Gutenberg-Richter recurrence parameters, particularly the activity rate parameter $a$, require correction to ensure consistency with the revised maximum magnitude values assigned to each branch, as the relationship between $a$, $b$, and $M_{\text{max}}$ must satisfy moment conservation principles [WEB:https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/Consequences-of-slip-rate-constraints-on-earthquake-Anderson-Luco/33242389b64fb368f9c2c57b80f1897f0279f1de]. The Anderson and Luco (1983) methodology provides a physically-based framework for this correction by deriving the activity rate parameter from fault slip rate constraints, ensuring that the cumulative seismic moment release implied by the recurrence relationship remains consistent with the geodetic or geological slip rate, thereby preventing overestimation of hazard at long return periods [WEB:https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/Consequences-of-slip-rate-constraints-on-earthquake-Anderson-Luco/33242389b64fb368f9c2c57b80f1897f0279f1de][WEB:https://academic.oup.com/jge/article/9/6/773/5127436]. For a fault classified as paleoseismic with limited contemporary activity, the slip rate assumption of 1 m/Myr itself warrants re-evaluation through a branched logic tree structure incorporating lower slip rates (0.1-0.5 m/Myr) that better reflect the sparse evidence of recent movement [KB:reitfontein.md]. An additional corrective approach involves incorporating separate branches that treat the Reitfontein Fault as part of the regional background seismicity zone rather than as a discrete fault source, effectively distributing the seismic potential across the broader stable continental region consistent with the observed diffuse seismicity pattern [KB:benchmark.md]. The revised logic tree should assign combined weights exceeding 0.70 to branches employing conservative characterizations (lower $M_{\text{max}}$ values, reduced slip rates, or background zone treatment) while retaining higher-hazard branches at reduced weights (≤0.30) to acknowledge residual epistemic uncertainty regarding the fault's potential for rare, large-magnitude events characteristic of stable continental region seismicity [KB:reitfontein.md][WEB:https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11069-022-05666-4]. Implementation of these corrective measures through a restructured logic tree framework will produce peak ground acceleration estimates more consistent with regional reference values while maintaining appropriate conservatism for engineering applications [KB:benchmark.md].
