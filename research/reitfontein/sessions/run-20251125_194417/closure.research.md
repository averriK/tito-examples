## Reitfontein Fault

The Reitfontein Fault is best classified as a paleoseismic structure rather than a neotectonic fault based on the absence of geological or geomorphological evidence of movement in the last 35,000 years and the presence of only minor present-day seismic activity. Active faults are typically defined as those that have produced historical earthquakes or evidence of movement within the last 35,000 years, and the Reitfontein Fault does not meet these established criteria [KB:reitfontein.md]. The characterization as an active fault with an assigned slip rate of 1 m/Myr represents a conservative engineering assumption rather than empirically demonstrated activity [KB:reitfontein.md]. The absence of paleoseismic investigations remains a significant data gap, but the lack of observable neotectonic features suggests that if the fault has been active in this timeframe, the activity rate is at or below detection limits of standard paleoseismic methods [KB:reitfontein.md][WEB:https://www.researchgate.net/publication/258773069_The_Seismotectonic_Model_of_Southern_Africa].
