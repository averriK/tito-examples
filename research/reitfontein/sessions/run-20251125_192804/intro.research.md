The objectives of the SRC study were twofold. First, the PSHA aimed to conform with the Global Industry Standard on Tailings Management (GISTM) requirements [KB:psha_deep_south.md]. Second, the study sought to provide an improved site-specific seismic characterization compared to that adopted in the 2008/2009 design of the Doornpoort TSF [KB:psha_deep_south.md]. The seismology work was conducted by the Seismology Research Centre (SRC), a division of ESS Earth Sciences Proprietary Limited, and documented in the report entitled "South Deep Gold Mine, Seismic hazard Assessment - June 2025 (Draft Report)" [KB:psha_deep_south.md].^[Verdict: TRUE, Confidence: HIGH, Rationale: The objectives of the SRC study are explicitly stated in the KB document psha_deep_south.md in the opening letter section. The SRC PSHA work produced Peak Ground Acceleration (PGA) values that were considerably higher than previously adopted values [KB:psha_deep_south.md][KB:introduction.md]. For the Doornpoort site, the PGA for a 475-year return period (10% probability of exceedance in 50 years) was estimated at 0.33 g, while the PGA for a 10,000-year return period was calculated as 1.3 g, assuming a shear wave velocity at 30 meters depth ($V_{s30}$) value of 600 m/s [KB:psha_deep_south.md]. These values represented a substantial increase from the previously adopted PGA values of 0.15 g for the 475-year return period and 0.25 g for the 10,000-year return period [KB:psha_deep_south.md]. The discrepancy between the seismic intensity values (PGA, PSA) provided by SRC and the regional reference values raised concerns regarding the potential impact on stability and deformation analyses, which would ultimately influence the type and extent of remediation measures required to manage such large ground motions [KB:psha_deep_south.md][KB:introduction.md].^[Verdict: TRUE, Confidence: HIGH, Rationale: All PGA values and comparisons are directly extracted from the KB documents. Given the specialized nature of the subject and the significant increase in predicted ground motions, SLR Consulting recommended that a third-party review be undertaken on the SRC PSHA work [KB:psha_deep_south.md]. Additionally, SLR recommended that the third-party reviewer should comment on the influence of mining-related seismicity on the modeling and the resultant PGA values [KB:psha_deep_south.md].
